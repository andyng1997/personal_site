I am a very active sports person and car enthusiast. When I'm not working, I'm usually doing one of the following...

Playing Badminton

Badminton is one of those sports that you don't see or hear too often about. I thought the same until I met my wife and she introduced me to the sport! I was mostly playing table tennis and soccer before but I met my wife through work and she invited me to try out badminton. At first I thought it was those backyard games that you play for fun but once I started playing, I realized how much more there is to the sport and how much fun it is.

I have been training and playing for nearly a year now and I can say that this sport requires alot of running and strength. I have done a few tournaments with my wife and while we do play competitively, we also have fun playing with our friends too.

Building/Fixing Cars

Ever since I was little, my parents didn't have much money. They were immigrants who came over from Vietnam with no other family in the US. I remember in my earliest childhood memories, I would always go along with my parents to get their cars fixed because since they didn't have much money, they couldn't afford a reliable car. Because of this, their cars kept breaking down and I would see that they have to spend so much money on repeairs and sometimes we didn't have enough money to eat.

I started to take an interest in cars and how they work so that when I was old enough, I would be able to save my parents money and fix cars on my own. When I turned 18, I got a job at a Toyota dealership with the help of my friend and after 2 years, I went from barely knowing how to change my oil to becoming an ASE Certified Master Diagnostic Mechanic.

Since then, I have not only taken an interest to fixing cars, I also like to modify and track my cars as well.

Gaming

When I was young, I was kind of shy. I didn't go outside as much and I would mostly enjoy playing video games. I had a nintendo gamecube when I was younger and I would always play games like Mario Kart or Pokemon during my free time. That would then eventually turn into much bigger games like Maplestory and Tera. Then when I was older, my friends invited me to play League of Legends with them. This was when the game first came out and I had never played a MOBA before.

After I played with them for a couple of hours, I was completely hooked onto the game. I spent thousands of hours grinding and playing ranked hoping to get better. Eventually I hit Diamond 1 which is less than the top 0.1% of all the players in the world and I was on my College's League of Legends team. We almost made it to the finals and was on the big stage as well.

Soon after I graduated college, I stopped playing video games as often as I did and I was more focused on getting a job and eventually saving up money to buy a house one day.
